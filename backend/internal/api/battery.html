Powering the beacon

How long does a beacon run?

Each room beacon is an ESP32-C6 that broadcasts Bluetooth non-stop. That radio + an always-awake CPU set a hard power floor — so battery life is measured in days, not months. Use the calculator to size a battery, or skip it all with USB. This decides the enclosure we 3D-print.

Why it isn't a coin-cell beacon

Always broadcasting

To be found instantly, the beacon advertises several times a second, around the clock. The radio never fully rests.

The CPU can't sleep

On this chip + toolchain, sleeping the CPU or dropping its clock stops the radio. So it runs flat-out while advertising — that's the cost floor.

Coin cells (CR2032) can't do it. They only supply a few mA — nowhere near 25 mA — so they're marked unusable. For real longevity, USB power or a dedicated low-power beacon chip (nRF52) is the answer; the ESP32-C6 trades battery life for Wi-Fi 6 we don't use here.

● Recommended

USB / wall power

A short cable to a USB adapter. Beacons sit under or on tables — usually near an outlet. Smallest case: just a clip and a cable notch.

Runtime: forever · case: tiny
Portable

Rechargeable LiPo

Cordless and clean, but recharge every few days. Case needs a battery bay + a charge port (TP4056). Bigger cell = bigger case = longer life.

Runtime: ~days · case: medium
Swappable

AA / AAA cells

No charging gear — just swap cells. Bulkiest case (a 2–3 cell holder), but anyone can service it. Good for spots with no outlet.

Runtime: ~days · case: large
