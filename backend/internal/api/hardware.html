Beacon hardware

Is there something better than the ESP32?

Short answer: yes. A chip built specifically for Bluetooth beacons sips roughly 1000× less power than our ESP32-C6 — and a couple of options may make the beacon problem disappear entirely. Here's the honest landscape, and what we might be missing.

The efficiency gap
~25 mA vs ~5 µA
What our ESP32-C6 draws advertising (it can spike to ~100 mA on TX) versus a Nordic nRF52 doing the exact same iBeacon. The nRF52's radio advertises while its CPU sleeps — the ESP32 can't, so it runs flat-out. That's the whole story.

You're not locked in. Everything we built speaks plain iBeacon (UUID · major · minor). So the beacon hardware is swappable with zero app or backend changes — an nRF52 module or a $5 beacon broadcasting the same identity just works. The chip choice is a hardware detail, not a rewrite.
If we keep building it · three paths
● For the demo

Keep the ESP32 — on USB

It already works, your firmware's done. Powered from a wall adapter or a room TV's USB port, battery life stops mattering. Zero new work.

Effort: none · life: forever (USB)
Build

Switch to nRF52

Re-flash the same iBeacon onto a Nordic nRF52 module (Holyiot, Seeed XIAO nRF52). ~4 years on a coin cell, tiny case, no charging. Arduino-friendly.

Effort: re-port firmware · life: ~years
Buy

Off-the-shelf iBeacon

Programmable nRF52 beacons (Holyiot ~$4, MINEW) — set UUID/major/minor in the vendor's phone app. 3–20 yr battery, pre-cased. No firmware, no 3D print.

Effort: config only · life: years
What we might not be seeing

You already have power at the spots

Rooms with TVs/monitors have spare USB; outlets sit near most tables. USB-power any chip and the whole battery question is moot — likely the simplest real answer.

UWB is far more precise — but overkill

Ultra-wideband (the iPhone's U1 chip) hits 10–30 cm vs BLE's 1–3 m. Great for exact location, unnecessary for "which room", and costly to install. Skip for now.

Passive check-in: NFC / QR

An NFC tag or QR sticker at the door is ~$0.20, zero power, forever — but a manual tap/scan, not "auto." A good fallback for spots with no outlet.

Accuracy isn't a chip problem

BLE bleeds through walls, so "which room" can misfire near edges. Your two-beacons-per-room idea + TX/threshold tuning fix that — no chip swap helps here.

Energy-harvesting beacons (future)

Solar/indoor-light beacons exist that never need a battery swap. Niche and pricier today, but a "never touch it" path worth knowing as the deployment grows.

For QuickRoom · my recommendation
  • Now / for the demo: keep the ESP32-C6, USB-powered. It works, it's reliable, no battery worry — smallest 3D-printed case.
  • For a wireless deployment: switch to nRF52 modules or buy programmable iBeacons — years on a coin cell, and zero software changes (same iBeacon).
  • Avoid: ESP32 + battery. Worst of both — bulky case, recharge every few days.
