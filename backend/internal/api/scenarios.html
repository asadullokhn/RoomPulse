The reality of room booking

The everyday "what if" cases

Meeting-room booking looks solved on a calendar and breaks the moment people walk around. These are the real situations — the ones that came up in the pitch, and more. First the problems, plainly.

Answers come next. How RoomPulse solves each of these will be added from the real system — not guesses. The problems are framed first so the solution speaks for itself.
Booking ≠ reality

Booked room is occupied

You booked a meeting room, but when you arrive, someone else is already inside. The system says it's yours; reality says "good luck knocking."

ProblemThe calendar booking has no idea who is actually in the room.
No-shows

Empty room is still marked booked

You need a room now. You walk past rooms that are physically empty, but the booking system shows every one of them as reserved.

ProblemPeople booked rooms and never showed up, so usable rooms stay blocked.
Overstay

The meeting that won't end

Your slot has started, but the previous group is still inside and in no hurry to leave. You hover outside, checking your watch.

ProblemBookings track the scheduled time, not when the room is actually free again.
Stale bookings

Ghost recurring holds

A weekly hold from a project that wrapped months ago still owns the room every Tuesday at 10. Nobody ever comes — but nobody can book it either.

ProblemStale recurring reservations quietly hoard rooms no one uses.
Real-time gap

The ten-minute room hunt

You just need somewhere for a quick call. You wander the floor opening doors, because the system shows everything booked and nothing about what's free right now.

ProblemThere's no live view of which rooms are actually available this minute.
Hoarding

Booked "just in case"

Someone reserves three slots across the day to be safe, uses one, and releases none. Multiply that by a team and the floor is "full" by 9am.

ProblemHoarding rooms costs nothing, so calendars fill with reservations no one honors.
Collisions

Two teams, one room

You walk in for your booking and another group is already setting up, equally certain the room is theirs. Now it's a standoff.

ProblemBookings and walk-ins collide with no single source of truth for who's really in.
Invisible use

Grabbed without booking

You take an empty room for a quick sync without booking it. Ten minutes in, someone with a reservation opens the door and you're out.

ProblemInformal, real use is invisible to the system, so it can't reflect or protect it.
Mismatch

Eight-person room for two

A quick 1:1 settles into the big room while a team of eight circles the floor with nowhere to land.

ProblemNothing matches the group that's actually there to the size of the room they took.
Lateness

You're a few minutes late

You arrive five minutes after the start — and your room has already been handed off, or flagged a no-show.

ProblemRigid no-show rules punish normal, everyday lateness.
Ghost presence

A laptop "holds" the room

The person stepped out, but their laptop (or phone) is still on the table — so the room reads as busy while it sits empty.

ProblemA device in the room isn't the same as a person actually using it.
Bad data

Nobody knows the real usage

Facilities sees rooms 90% "booked" and plans a new floor — while half of them sit empty most afternoons.

ProblemBookings aren't reality, so every utilization number built on them is fiction.
