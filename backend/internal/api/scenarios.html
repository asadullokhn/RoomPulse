The "what if" questions, answered

RoomPulse is built so people never think about checking in or out — presence does it, with grace timers so a blip doesn't bounce you and a safety net so a dead phone can't strand a room. Here's exactly what happens in the awkward cases, including what we deliberately haven't built yet.

Scenarios
What if…Handled automatically

I'm late to check in

There's no window to miss. The moment you walk in and your phone sees the room's beacon, you're checked in — on time or 40 minutes late.

  1. You walk in (whenever)
  2. Phone detects the beacon, ~2–4 s of being near
  3. You're checked in — dashboard & booking update
Planned: auto-release a no-show's booking if nobody arrives by N minutes after the start.
What if…Handled automatically

I step out for a short break

A quick signal dip won't bounce you — there's a 6-second grace. Step away longer and the room frees; walk back and you're checked in again in seconds.

  1. You leave → 6 s grace (ignores brief flaps)
  2. Still gone → checked out, room is freed
  3. You return → ~2–4 s → checked back in
Note: a break does free the room. A "hold my room for X min" grace is an easy future tweak.
What if…The safety net

I leave without checking out

You never check out by hand — it happens for you, three ways, so a lost message or dead battery can't strand a room as "occupied."

  1. App open → 6 s after you leave → checked out
  2. App closed → iOS region-exit → checked out
  3. Phone dead/offline → 90 s reap releases it
What if…Handled automatically

I'm between two rooms / near a wall

It picks the nearest room by signal strength, waits for ~2 steady readings before committing, and ignores brief flaps — so you don't flicker between rooms.

  1. Phone ranges every beacon in earshot
  2. Strongest signal above the threshold wins
  3. 2 steady samples → commit (no flicker)
Tip: big rooms can run two beacons for even coverage — both map to the same room.
What if…Tracked · with a caveat

A friend checks in to my booked room first

The live headcount shows your friend by name — that's accurate. But the booking flips to "in use" when anyone enters; today it means "room in use," not "the booker is here."

  1. Friend enters → headcount adds their name
  2. Booking marked checked-in (room is in use)
  3. You arrive → you're added to the headcount too
Planned: match the booking's check-in to the actual person who reserved it.
What if…Tracked · with a caveat

I leave my phone in the room

The system follows the phone, not the person — so the room stays occupied until the phone leaves. The 90 s safety net can't help here: the phone is still online and reporting in.

  1. Phone keeps reporting "I'm in this room"
  2. Room stays checked-in (no exit signal)
  3. Frees only when the phone physically leaves
Planned: release at the booking's end time, or add a motion/occupancy sensor.
What we deliberately haven't built yet — so we can answer honestly when asked:
  • No-show release — free a booking if nobody arrives after the start.
  • Booker identity — verify the check-in is the person who reserved, not just "someone's here."
  • Phone-left-behind — release at the booking's end time, or with a presence sensor.
Everything else above works today.
